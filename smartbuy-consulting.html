Product Design

SmartBuy Consulting

Full product design for a procurement consulting startup — from zero to a clear brand identity, information architecture, and a website that communicates what the business does and builds trust with potential clients.

TypeClient Project
RoleUX Designer + Brand Design + Front-end
ToolsFigma, HTML, CSS

01 — Problem

A startup with no identity and no clear message

SmartBuy was a procurement consulting company just getting started. They had a business idea and a team, but no visual identity, no defined positioning, and no clarity on how to communicate what they actually do to potential clients.

The challenge wasn't just designing a website — it was helping the client figure out who they are first, then translating that into a digital product that builds credibility and converts visitors.

Starting point

  • No logo, no brand colors, no visual identity
  • Client had no clear positioning or defined message
  • No existing website or digital presence
  • Multiple services with no hierarchy or structure
02 — Research & Discovery

Understanding the business before designing anything

Before touching Figma, I ran several meetings with the client to understand their vision, their target clients, and what differentiated them from other consulting firms. The key insight: SmartBuy wasn't just a purchasing agency — they combined strategic procurement expertise with AI-powered analysis tools.

I also researched competitor consulting websites to understand what information architecture worked in this space, what users expected to find, and how to build credibility in a B2B context.

What the client wanted to communicate

  • Strategic procurement expertise
  • Concrete, measurable results (cost reduction, efficiency)
  • Adaptability to each client's structure and culture
  • Use of AI and data analysis tools

What users needed to understand quickly

  • What SmartBuy does and for whom
  • Why choose them over competitors
  • What specific services they offer
  • How to get in touch
03 — Brand Identity

Building the identity from scratch

With the business direction defined, I designed the full visual identity — logo, color palette, typography, and visual language. The brand needed to communicate professionalism, trust, and growth. The result was a clean, corporate system anchored in deep navy blue.

Final logo — "Transformamos la gestión de compras en una ventaja competitiva estratégica"

Brand presentation slide — Who we are and our vision

Structuring the content before designing screens

05 — Design Decisions

Key decisions made throughout the process

What was decided and why

  • Deep navy blue as primary color — communicates trust, authority, and professionalism in B2B
  • Single page layout — reduces friction for a B2B audience that wants quick answers
  • Benefits section before services — leads with value before explaining the how
  • Clear CTA in the hero — "Contactanos" visible without scrolling
  • Minimal copy, structured in scannable blocks — B2B users don't read, they scan

What was discarded

  • Multi-page site — unnecessary complexity for an early-stage consulting business
  • Case studies section — client had no public cases to show yet
  • Blog or resources section — out of scope for MVP launch
06 — Result

From zero to a live, credible digital presence

The final deliverable was a fully responsive website that clearly communicates SmartBuy's offer, builds trust through consistent visual identity, and gives potential clients a clear path to get in touch.
